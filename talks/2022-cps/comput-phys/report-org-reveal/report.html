Appendix: Mathematics Proof of DIA (2D Integral)

\begin{eqnarray} &&I_{2D}=\int_0^{a_1}dx\int_0^{a_2}dye^{-U(x,y)},\\ &&X'(x)=\int_0^xe^{-U(\xi,0)}d\xi, Y'(y)=\int_0^ye^{-U(0,\xi)}d\xi.\\ &&X'(0)=0, Y'(0)=0\\ &&a'_1=\int_0^{a_1}e^{-U(\xi,0)}d\xi,a'_2=\int_0^{a_2}e^{-U(0,\xi)}d\xi\\ &&I_{2D}=\int_0^{a'_1}dX'\int_0^{a'_2}dY'e^{-F(X',Y')}.\\ &&\text{If U(x,y) has a minimum at the origin (x=0, y=0) and U(0,0)=0},\\ &&F(0,0)=0,\frac{\partial F}{\partial X'}{\Big |}_0=\frac{\partial F}{\partial Y'}{\Big |}_0=0\\ &&I_{2D}\simeq a'_1a'_2. \end{eqnarray}
